We set out exploring the landscape of people who actually knew how to build engineering projects. What we found was discouraging.

Most of them were bluffing — reciting concepts they barely understood, unable to explain their own work when pressed. The ecosystem around final-year projects was filled with people gatekeeping half-knowledge, delivering black-box solutions that left students confused and exposed during viva.

Here's what we realised: a project isn't just a showcase. It's proof of knowledge. If a student can't explain what they built, the project has failed — regardless of how impressive it looks.

So we planned something different. We'd be transparent. We'd teach professionally from the basics. We'd build systems that students could actually understand, replicate, and defend.

Zeus IoT was designed around one idea: make learning through projects easier, accessible, and genuinely educational. Not just for the submission. For the knowledge.
